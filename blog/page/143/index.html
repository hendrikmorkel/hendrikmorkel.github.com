Snippet: Remote Exposure Book

Aug 18th, 2011


“Cameras don’t make pictures, photographers do.”
Got this book on Tuesday and have since implemented my morning/ afternoon one hour reading sessions. It is full of useful advice on how to improve your photography skills, and it is made with us hiking, backpacking and mountaineering folks in mind. Thus if you’re serious about photography, and before you invest more money into new fancy camera gear, first spend the ~ $17 for Remote Exposure: A Guide to Hiking and Climbing Photography.

In the meanwhile, I am working on an article about the Sony NEX-5 and Panasonic Lumix GF2, which hopefully is ready next week. So after you’ve read the book, you can read about which lightweight camera you might want to get =)

Introducing: Snippets

Aug 16th, 2011

Snippets?

Yes, Snippets.

What are Snippets? ¹

Snippets will be short posts, consisting of two paragraphs max, a link, a photo, a quote, or a mix thereof. A Snippet post will be what is too long for Twitter, too short for a proper blog post. Quality is still what drives these, so don’t yet unsubscribe - I will limit these Snippet posts, so don’t expect five daily updates. Quality over quantity is still my motto, and the thought is to remove unnecessary clutter, simplify and streamline posts to the necessary bits - much like reducing your carried gear to the necessary stuff. Snippets!

What content will Snippets contain? Things which are on my mind, breaking news, links to interesting articles I read, and whatever else I stumble across which I think will add value to your reading experience and the worldwide UL community.



¹ Those who know John Gruber’s excellent Daring Fireball or Marcel Wichmann’s UARRR.org blog will be familiar with the concept of Snippets. Others might want to look up the Wikipedia definition of a Snippet.
