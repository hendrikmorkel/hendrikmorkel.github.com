Winter Backpacking Basics Course

Nov 22nd, 2012

The Winter Backpacking Basics Course is aimed at backpackers who have backpacked in Spring, Summer and Autumn and now would like to gather the experience to enjoy the cold season in a safe, warm and lightweight manner.

Winter is a magic time to be backpacking in the forests and hills: The world is transformed into a white paradise, in which you’re leaving your own trail and cross the tracks of Lynx, Elk, and Hare while you walk through this silent world in which the nights are long and the days are short & cold. While this can be a dangerous time of the year, in this course you’ll get access to the knowledge and skills needed to be safe and comfortable in winter.

Among other things, in this course you learn how to

  • Select appropriate gear for a winter trip
  • Introduction to XC skiing or snowshoeing as our mode of transportation
  • Stay warm, dry and comfortable while snowshoeing or skiing and at camp
  • Pitching your shelter in the snow
  • Skills like melting snow with different stove systems, evaluating the thickness of ice on lakes

Itinerary of this course:
Day 1:
- Meeting in the early morning and travel together to the starting point of our hike
- Hike for two to four hours before we start to look for a good place to camp
- Preparation of camp, pitching shelters

Day 2:
- Full day of hiking
- Navigating in the snowy forest
- Building a fire

Day 3:
- We pack up and hike out
- Post-trip meal, optional sauna

After this course you will possess the skills and knowledge to plan your own winter weekend trips and will feel confident to hike & camp in the cold.
